Hungarian Wedding Traditions

Hungarian weddings are rich in tradition, often spanning several days and filled with vibrant customs. From the "taking of the bride" (menyasszony kikérése) where the groom's party negotiates for the bride, to the lively folk dances and the midnight "menyasszonytánc" (bride's dance) where guests pay to dance with the bride, every moment is steeped in cultural significance. Food plays a central role, with a multi-course feast and traditional dishes like goulash and stuffed cabbage. The celebration often culminates with the bride changing into a red and white dress, symbolizing her new status as a married woman. We invite you to experience a taste of these beautiful traditions with us!

Music & Dancing

Music is the soul of a Hungarian wedding, and we plan to honor that tradition with plenty of it! But as many of you know, we also share a deep love for salsa. So, get your dancing shoes ready for a vibrant mix of traditional folk music, classic wedding hits, and the infectious rhythms of Latin dance. Taking of the Bride (address & time will be shared)

The day starts at Evelin’s home, where family and friends will gather. This is a lively tradition, with little challenges along the way, sometimes for the groom and his family, who come to take Evelin to the church. There will also be a short goodbye speech from Evelin’s home, marking the start of this special day. It’s a symbolic send-off from her home and a way for everyone to celebrate the beginning of the wedding.

Cultural Background

We’ll be following Hungarian traditions, as Evelin’s family is part of the Hungarian minority in Romania. These traditions have been passed down through generations and are a way to celebrate family, community, and cultural identity.

Historically, the Hungarian minority has lived in parts of Romania for centuries, keeping their language, customs, and unique celebrations alive despite changing borders and political climates.

We’re also adding a few French touches to honor Guilhem’s heritage and blend our two cultures together.
